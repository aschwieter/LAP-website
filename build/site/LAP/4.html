Home Health Quick Reference

Home Health

Admission to HHC services

  • Meet with the patient and caregivers to identify interpreter needs across the spectrum of HHC services. Document these preferences as applicable (case communication and care plan).

  • If applicable, discuss when patient/family/friends may interpret and complete form J1011.

  • Provide resources in preferred language as able: direct interpreter line phone number, HHC folder, consents, documents, Knowing Notes, and instructions.

  • As able, request urgent translation for critical home care procedure instructions.

  • Plan ahead to request in person interpreter for SOC visits as available.

Working with live interpreters in the home

  • As they are available, prioritize live interpreters for start of care (SOC) visits, teaching procedures, and in areas of known poor cellular reception.

  • Plan to meet outside of the home and enter and leave together when possible.

  • Notify interpreter services of a changed or cancelled visit.

  • Plan in advance when scheduling rare language interpreter visits.

Working with a phone or video interpreter while in the home.

  • Use phone or video interpreters when a live interpreter is not available.

  • Use speaker phone as able to limit handling of phone during hands on training or care.
